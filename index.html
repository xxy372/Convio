The Problem

Being there,
when you can't be there

Concerts are becoming harder to access: cost, travel, time, distance, and accessibility barriers. For many people, watching from home is the only realistic option, but today's livestreams rarely feel shared, immersive, or alive.

Existing livestream platforms make concerts viewable, but they still feel passive and distant. They do not recreate the energy of being in the same space, reacting in the moment, or experiencing a performance together with friends.

Convio is a Mixed Reality concert experience that explores a more social and immersive alternative. Users can choose a show, place a virtual blanket and stage in their own space, and enjoy the concert from home in a way that feels more present and interactive.

The experience can be enjoyed solo or online with friends. In shared mode, users can see one another as avatars, hear each other through live voice chat, and interact with the concert through hand-drawn visual effects and other immersive elements.

This version is a headset-based proof of concept. Our long-term vision is to bring the experience to XR glasses, making it lighter, more comfortable, and more natural for everyday concert experiences.

The Experience

How Convio works

A shared Mixed Reality concert experience that brings live-feeling performances into your own space, whether you want to enjoy them alone or with friends.

Choose a Show
Users begin by selecting the performance they want to experience, making the concert feel intentional and personal from the very start.
Place the Stage in Your Space
A virtual blanket and stage can be placed wherever the user chooses, turning an everyday room into a concert setting that feels immersive, flexible, and close.
Start the Concert
Once the blanket is placed, the performance begins. This creates a simple and intuitive flow, helping users move naturally from setup into the experience.
Share the Experience with Friends
Users can join online with friends to watch together in real time, making the experience feel more social, connected, and alive than a traditional livestream.
See and Talk to Each Other
In online mode, users can see one another as Meta default avatars and communicate through live voice chat, creating a sense of presence and shared attendance.
Interact Through Visual Effects
Users can draw visual effects with their hands during the performance, adding a playful and expressive layer of interaction. More concert-based VFX are planned as the project develops.

Research & Process

From idea to prototype

A semester of discovery, pivoting, and building — guided by user research and a commitment to designing for those who need it most.

Apr 2025
Funding
Frost Research Grant
Secured initial funding and established the original vision of a shared AR concert platform for geographically dispersed friends.
Jul–Aug 2025
Discovery
Concept Formation & Proposals
Defined research questions, set goals, met with advisors Dr. Millet, Dr. Chuan, and Prof. Martens. Locked mobile + AR as core platform. Submitted three iterated proposals.
Sep–Oct 2025
Research & Pivot
Focus Group to Audience Pivot
Ran a 90-minute focus group with 6 participants. The "Social Highs and Lows" exercise revealed that AR concerts are most valuable for those who cannot attend in person — reshaping our entire target audience.
Oct–Nov 2025
Design & Build
Prototype Development in Unity
Built avatar creation, blanket placement, Photon Fusion multiplayer networking, synchronized music playback, spatialized voice chat, and a tap-to-fireworks collective mechanic.
Nov 2025
Testing
User Testing — 8 Participants
4 sessions of 2 participants each at University of Miami. Validated core mechanics. Added self-view window and AR coordinate calibration phase based on feedback.
